Where Funding Support Fits

Funding is not the core focus of the Plansale website. We position it as a practical support layer for businesses that are planning digital growth work and want help understanding which programs may align with their project scope, eligibility, and documentation needs.

In the right situation, funding or financing can help reduce upfront pressure for projects such as website development, e-commerce implementation, booking systems, CRM adoption, software upgrades, analytics setup, and selected marketing initiatives. We help clients review options, prepare materials, and connect project planning with application readiness.

FEATURED PROGRAM

Ontario RAISE Grant

Supporting Indigenous, Black, and racialized entrepreneurs in Ontario with funding, training, and mentorship opportunities.

Up to $10,000

RAISE Grant Program

One-time grant for eligible entrepreneurs

The RAISE (Randall Activation, Innovation and Scale-up for Entrepreneurs) Grant provides $10,000 to Indigenous, Black, and other racialized entrepreneurs in Ontario after completing program requirements including training, coaching, and networking sessions.

  • $10,000 non-repayable grant upon program completion
  • Business training and coaching from industry experts
  • Networking opportunities with fellow entrepreneurs
  • Eligible costs: Marketing, digital transformation, equipment, operations

Eligibility Requirements

  • Self-identify as Indigenous, Black, or racialized entrepreneur
  • Business located and operating in Ontario
  • Complete mandatory training and coaching sessions
  • Commit to program participation requirements

LOAN PROGRAM

Canada Small Business Financing Program

Government-backed loans to help small businesses purchase equipment and improve facilities.

Up to $1 Million

Small Business Financing Program (CSBFP)

Government-guaranteed business loans

The Canada Small Business Financing Program makes it easier for small businesses to get loans from financial institutions by sharing the risk with lenders. Use funds to purchase equipment, improve leasehold improvements, or buy real property.

  • Loan amounts: Up to $1 million total ($350k for equipment)
  • Government guarantee: 85% loan guarantee reduces lender risk
  • Flexible terms: Up to 10 years for equipment, 15 for real property
  • Competitive rates: Prime + 3% floating or fixed rate options

Eligible Businesses

  • Gross annual revenues of $10 million or less
  • Operating for profit in Canada
  • Most industries eligible (some exceptions apply)

Frequently Asked Questions

Practical questions about funding support for digital growth projects

What kinds of projects may fit this page?

Typical examples include websites, e-commerce stores, booking systems, CRM adoption, analytics setup, software implementation, local SEO foundations, and broader digital transformation work. Eligibility always depends on the specific program and your business situation.

Do you guarantee approval?

No. Funding decisions are always made by the program provider or lender. Our role is to help you identify potentially relevant options, organize documentation, clarify project scope, and present your application more clearly and professionally.

Can funding cover SEO, websites, or digital marketing?

Some programs may support website development, software, e-commerce, training, technology upgrades, or selected digital initiatives. Coverage varies by program, so the first step is matching your project scope to the right funding or financing pathway.

Is this your main service?

No. Plansale is primarily focused on websites, SEO, Google Ads, social media, landing pages, and digital growth strategy. Funding support is offered as an additional resource when a client project may benefit from it.

How do you usually help?

We usually start by reviewing your business goals, project type, and timeline. Then we help define the project scope, identify potentially relevant programs, prepare supporting information, and align the funding discussion with a practical implementation plan.

What is the next step?

The next step is a consultation to understand whether your business is exploring a website, e-commerce, SEO, software, or broader digital growth project, and whether funding support is worth pursuing alongside it.
